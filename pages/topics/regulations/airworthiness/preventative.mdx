# Preventative Maintenance

According to <Reg showTitle part="1.1" />, preventative maintenance simple or
minor preservation operations and the replacement of small standard parts not
involving complex assembly operations.

Any certificated pilot may perform preventive maintenance on any aircraft owned
or operated by that pilot, so long as it is not being operated for part 121,
129, or 135 operations. - <Reg part="43.3" />

Allowed items of preventative maintenance are listed and limited to the items of
[14 CFR 43 Appendix A(c)](<https://ecfr.federalregister.gov/current/title-14/chapter-I/subchapter-C/part-43/appendix-Appendix%20A%20to%20Part%2043#p-Appendix-A-to-Part-43(c)>).

For instance, the following are allowed as preventative maintenance items:

- Removal, installation, and repair of landing gear tires
- Lubrication not requiring disassembly other than removal of nonstructural
  items
- Replacing safety belts
- Replacing bulbs, reflectors, and lenses of position and landing lights
- Replacing and servicing batteries
- Cleaning or replacing fuel and oil strainers or filter elements
