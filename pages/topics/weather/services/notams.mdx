# Notices to Airmen (NOTAMs)

Notices to Airmen (NOTAMs) are time-sensitive information pertinent to pilots
that could not be published ahead of time. They typically include:

- Runway closures
- NAVAID availability
- ILS status
- Obstacles
- Radar service disruption

## Types of NOTAMs

- (D) NOTAMS: Information that requires mass dissemination
  - Most NOTAMs are D-NOTAMs
  - Usually pertains to enroute NAVAIDs or public-use airports, facilities and
    procedures
  - Contains a keyword which identifies what it pretains to, including:
    - RWY
    - TWY
    - RAMP
    - APRON
    - AD
    - OBST
    - NAV
    - COM
    - SVC
    - AIRSPACE
    - U (unverified)
    - O (other)
- FDC NOTAMS: IFR-related information pertaining to IFR charts, procedures, or
  airspace
- POINTER NOTAMS: Call outs to other NOTAMs
- MILITARY NOTAMS: Information pertaining to military NAVAIDS or airfields
- SAA NOTAMS: Pertains to Special Activity Airspace being active outside of its
  published schedule
- FICON NOTAMS: Related to the condition of asphalt surfaces
