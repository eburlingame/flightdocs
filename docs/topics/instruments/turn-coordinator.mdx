# Turn Coordinator

A traditional turn coordinator is essential a combination of two instruments: a
rate-of-turn indicator, and an inclinometer.

## Inclinometer

An inclinometer shows the quality or coordination of a turn, either slipping or
skidding.

![Slipping and Skidding](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/1b/Turn_coordinators-en.svg)

## Turn-and-slip Indicators vs Turn Coordinators

![Turn-and-slip and turn coordinators](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/0c/Turn_indicators.png)

Both the turn and slip indicator and turn coordinator are gyroscopic
instruments, but they differ slightly in what they display.

The turn coordinator is a gyroscopic instrument which displays roll rate _and_
rate of turn. The turn-and-slip indicator only shows rate-of turn.
