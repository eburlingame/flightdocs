# Commercial Pilot Privileges and Limitations

The holder of a commercial pilot certificate may act as pilot in command of an
aircraft for **compensation or hire**, provided the person is qualified in
accordance with this part and with the applicable parts of this chapter that
apply to the operation.

- A commercial pilot is free to work _for_ a
  [commercial operator](/docs/topics/regulations/commercial/operators), who is certificated for
  the operations they perform.
- They may also act _as_ an operator themselves, but the services their offer
  may require a [operating certificate](/docs/topics/regulations/commercial/operators).

Limitations:

- Without an instrument rating, a commercial pilot may not carry passengers:
  - At night
  - On cross-country flights in excess of **50nm**
- Cannot carry passengers for hire in formation flight. -
  <Reg part="91.111" />
- Cannot carry passengers for hire in a restricted, limited, or experimental
  aircraft. (<Reg part="91.313" />, <Reg part="91.315" />,
  <Reg part="91.317" />, <Reg part="91.319" />)
